Podcasto — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 4, 2026

Introduction

Podcasto is a free, open-source podcast player for Android developed by ghostwan. This privacy policy explains how Podcasto handles your data. In short: we do not collect, store, or share any personal data.

Data Stored Locally

All your data is stored locally on your device and never leaves it unless you explicitly choose to back it up. This includes:

  • Podcast subscriptions and episode metadata
  • Playback progress and listening history
  • Playlists, tags, and bookmarks
  • Downloaded episodes (audio files)
  • App settings and preferences

Network Requests

Podcasto makes network requests solely to provide its core functionality:

  • iTunes Search API — to search and discover podcasts. Queries are sent to Apple's servers.
  • RSS feeds — to fetch podcast episode listings directly from podcast publishers.
  • Audio streaming — to stream or download episode audio files from podcast publishers' servers.
  • Podcast artwork — to load cover images from podcast publishers' servers.

No personal data, device identifiers, or usage analytics are sent with these requests.

Optional Features

The following features involve third-party services and are entirely opt-in:

Google Drive Backup

If you choose to sign in with your Google account, Podcasto can back up your data (subscriptions, episodes, tags, bookmarks, playlist, history, settings) to your Google Drive App Data folder. This data is only accessible by Podcasto and by you. No data is shared with ghostwan or any third party. You can revoke access at any time from your Google Account permissions.

Gemini AI Discovery

If you provide a Gemini API key, Podcasto sends your subscription list to Google's Generative AI API to generate podcast recommendations. Your API key is stored locally on your device and is never shared with ghostwan. Usage of the Gemini API is subject to Google's API Terms of Service.

Built-in Web Server

Podcasto includes an optional web server that allows you to control the app from a web browser. When enabled, it runs locally on your device. If you enable the public tunnel feature (via localhost.run), your web interface becomes accessible from the internet. You can protect it with a password. No data is sent to ghostwan through this feature.

Data Collection

Podcasto does not:

  • Collect any personal information
  • Track your usage or behavior
  • Display advertisements
  • Use analytics services or crash reporting tools
  • Share any data with third parties
  • Require account creation

Data Deletion

Since all data is stored locally on your device, you can delete all Podcasto data at any time by:

  • Clearing the app's data from Android Settings
  • Uninstalling the app

If you used Google Drive backup, you can delete the backup data from your Google Drive.

Children's Privacy

Podcasto is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect any data from children.

Changes to This Policy

This privacy policy may be updated occasionally. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. The policy is also available in the app's source code repository.

Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy, you can open an issue on the GitHub repository.

Open Source

Podcasto is open-source software. You can review the complete source code at github.com/ghostwan/Podcasto to verify these privacy claims.
